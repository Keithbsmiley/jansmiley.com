Colonia, Uruguay

By Jan | Published: January 21, 2008
I have the incredible opportunity of traveling to South America. After an overnight flight to Buenos Aires, Argentina, a cab ride, and then a ferry across the Rio de la Plata, my daughter and I arrived in Colonia, Uruguay. 
Fortunately, I actually did get some sleep on the plane, so I was able to enjoy walking around this UNESCO World Heritage site. I’m happy to share some of my favorite photos with you. If you enjoy these, please sign up to subscribe to my blog feed, or check back regularly, as I plan to share the rest the rest of our trip with you over the next couple of weeks.


 


Please tell me I’m not the only one who takes closeup pictures of peeling paint and crumbling walls when I travel?
I love the variety of merchandise this shop has on display on their sidewalk. The patchwork was actually sewn from a plush fabric.
We just happened to come across this lovely area with lots of arts and crafts shops. Lush both outside and in!


The tile museum was closed when we finally found it, but I couldn’t resist photographing the door handle.

The people in Colonia were gracious and the setting was beautiful. I’d definitely go back there if the opportunity presents itself. I’d love to explore more of Uruguay.

Time for a New Journal

By Jan | Published: January 19, 2008
It’s time.
My current journal is full.
I have to start a new one.

I have a love/hate relationship with a new journal. 
I love holding a full journal that I’ve bonded with over it’s time with me. 
I hate having a journal full of blank pages staring at me. 
I love the options, potential, possibilities and excitement of covering a new journal. 
I hate blank journal pages staring at me. 
I’m taking a deep breath and reminding myself that I’ll bond with my new journal. 
Someday. 
Right now I want to take a pause and look back at recent journals that I bonded with.
My most recent journal had it’s own pouch that I embroidered for it.
I recycled a pencil pouch from one of my husband’s old briefcases. 
I love to recycle – and recycling fun stuff like leather pencil pockets is even better!
See how much character a journal has when it’s being used, enjoyed, and filled up? 
Have I mentioned how much I like my journals after I’ve bonded with them?
This former journal was covered with some great leftover quilt pieces, and some reclaimed leather.  Texture, my favorite palette, and comfortable to hold. 
This journal and I bonded in Italy a couple of years ago.
That tuscan gold paint on the spine? I remember when I spilled that 
when I was in Rome……ahhhh, visual memories.
For this former journal, I recycled a wool sweater. 
I embroidered with some pearl cotton and then dug through 
my grandmother’s button box (one of my prized possessions), to add to the spine. 
I had fun choosing beads to use on the end of the leather tie. 
I really loved carrying this journal.

So, here’s what’s facing me now: A Blank Journal. 
I’d like to bond with it, I really would.     
Wish me luck?

Where's the Pause Button?

By Jan | Published: January 7, 2008

You read that right – I’m looking for the pause button. 
The one that will allow me to take a deep breath and, well, pause.

The remote control for the DVD player has a pause button……
My iPod has a pause button……
Red lights make me pause when I’m driving…….
Waiting in lines actually make one pause……
What I woke up this morning thinking about was finding Life’s Pause Button. I’m not asking everything around me to stop, I’m not asking for anyone else to slow down, I just want the ability to hit pause every once in a while, until I catch my breath. 2008! How did it get to be 2008 already???

I’m not feeling evolved enough at this moment to get into an articulate discussion on being present in the moment, or a discussion on mindfulness, or having soulful moments. I don’t need a reminder that we all need to slow down and smell the roses.  My brain knows all that, and I expect you know that too. Our lives are so full with non-stop activities, to-do lists, meetings, errands, constant stimuli, and internal and external pressures to “do more”. 
Because honestly, there will always be more to be done. Always. Acknowledging that no matter how hard I work, how many hours I concentrate, or how good my intentions, I’ll never get it all done. And that’s really OK. I just need to remind myself of that every now and then. 
What I need is a way to remind myself to take a pause whenever I need to. A deep breath, a relaxing of my shoulders, an appreciation for my life and opportunities, and a great big exhale. 
My steaming cup of coffee in front of a warm fire, with the sunlight streaming through the window was my morning’s pause button. 
What reminds you to enjoy a pause? I’d love to hear your comments.

Fingerless Mittens – Car Knitting Time

By Jan | Published: December 4, 2007

I had a long drive last weekend to and from a conference. Lucky for me, someone else was driving, so I had time to knit. I made one of these on the way to the conference and the other one on the way home.
I’ve been riding Charlotte’s new light rail system to get to the McColl Center, and these fingerless gloves will be great for getting money out of my pocket, and for keeping track of my ticket. Last week I kept taking my gloves off and setting them down on the seat next to me, so I’m lucky I haven’t already lost some!
I mostly followed the Maine Morning Mittens pattern available for free here. It’s one of the patterns in The Book of Yarn, which I borrowed from the library last week. 
The yarn is a superwash wool, which I bought in a natural color. I dyed it using Wilton cake dyes. It was really easy to do and I’m pleased with how the yarn and the mittens turned out.

Journal Quilt

By Jan | Published: November 6, 2007

I participated in the 2007 Journal Quilt exhibit which debuted at the International Quilt Festival in Houston, Texas. Here’s my entry into the exhibit.

I experimented with how to incorporate a digital photograph without using the photo “as is”. I played with Photoshop Elements to alter the photograph. It’s amazing what a deadline will do for me…… I had Photoshop Elements loaded onto my computer, but had not taken any time to play with it and see what it could do. I had great fun making it. It made me start thinking about other ways that I can use my photographs, other than in my travel journals. These images were printed directly onto canvas, in case you were wondering.

I had so much fun making this piece that I’ve already finished another one using the same photograph, and have a 3rd piece (with a different photo) ready to quilt and bind later this week. I’ll be sure to post the finished piece.

Thanks for checking in!

Catching Up

By Jan | Published: October 2, 2007

It’s been a busy week. A newly finished piece, some yarn time, some studio events, and a workshop. Here’s a snippet of each:

Open House
The band, Work Release, played upstairs in my studio during last Friday’s Open House. Here they are just as they were getting started. See how well I cleaned up and made room for them to play and for people to mill around?

It was Great to have them playing and everyone remarked how much they enjoyed hearing them. A Huge Thanks to Angela, John, Julie, and Mike for coming and playing. If you’re in the Charlotte area and want to contact them to play at an event, just e-mail me and I’ll give you their info.

Yarn
I’ve been playing with yarn during my evening relaxation time. Here’s a mohair and silk wrap I’m currently working on. I hope to wear it to an October event, so I’m snatching bits of time from everywhere to try and get it done.



New Piece – Sienna
I finished a new piece last week. If you’ve been to my blog before you’ve probably seen it in progress. I think with my class in Italy on my brain, these colors will just keep showing up in my work. I titled it Sienna. It just reminds me so much of Italy. For more info on my class in Cortona, click on the Italy button on the right of your screen.



Workshop
The Mint Museum of Craft and Design sponsored a workshop with Dorothy Caldwell last Saturday. I’ve long admired Dorothy’s work, and enjoyed the class. The class focused on hand stitching. Here’s part of the workshop description: “This workshop will examine “the stitch” based on the colorful tradition of Indian Kantha embroidery. This 500 year old tradition of West Bengal consists of small silk and muslin remnants layered, pieced and embroidered with elaborate motifs created by a combination of the running stitch and the chain stitch. Participants will learn the techniques involved and move on to develop their own imagery.” It’s been a long time since I’ve taken a class and I really enjoyed the day. Here’s one of the stitched pieces I did as I continue to explore circles.


Well, back to work I go. Thanks for checking in!
