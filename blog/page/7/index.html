Full Circle: Red 1-4

By Jan | Published: May 25, 2007

I’m getting ready to head out for a week’s vacation with my family.

I wanted to get some new work to the framer before I headed out. Here’s some of my new work that was waiting for me when I got back:


These are called Full Circle: Red 1-4. I’m really enjoying the opportunity to work with different materials. These are recycled wools, wool roving (unspun wool fibers), yarn, and silk sari fibers. They are shadow box framed and are each about 15″ square.

I have a larger needle felted piece that I made before these pieces, but I haven’t been able to get a decent photograph of it yet. I’ll post that piece when I get a good shot. Anyway, I really like these pieces.

Needle felting is one of the processes I want to work on during my time at the McColl. It’s so much fun to experiment and see what I can get the materials to do. The needle felting machine I’m using is from here. Thanks Bob & Laura!

Open Studio Saturday

By Jan | Published: May 24, 2007

One of the opportunities (and requirements) of my residency at the McColl Center is that the artists are all required to be in their studios working on scheduled Open Studio Saturdays. These Saturdays are an additional time for the public to come visit the gallery spaces in the building and wander through our studios, seeing what we’re working on, and asking questions. It’s a lot of fun – but so far, unpredictable. There might be a lot of people, so focusing on your work is challenging, or there might be just a few people, so you wonder what you’re doing there all day.

On one of our Open Studio Saturdays, my wonderful 19 year old daughter Emma came with me. She worked on updating my e-mail addresses (on my old PowerBook, may it rest in peace), and had fun playing with my camera. I was working on one of my mixed media pieces when she decided playing with angled camera shots would be entertaining.

Here’s the first mixed media piece I started and finished at the McColl Center.


It’s titled Aerial View, and it is 14″ x 14″, wood, canvas, paint, fabric, metal, paper, stamping, collage, printing, stitching, and dyeing.

I called it Aerial View because it reminds me of that view you get of agricultural areas, and actually of city grids, too, when you’re looking out of an airplane window, and also because my belltower studio windows are up pretty high. Not a true aerial view high, but about 4-5 stories high.

I’d love to hear if it reminds you of something different.

If you’re ever in the Charlotte area, please check the calendar, or contact me to come by for a visit.

Computer Woes

By Jan | Published: May 23, 2007

I spent a lot of time with David, the McColl tech guru, working on my Mac laptop. My mail program had been giving me some trouble, and we thought part of the problem was so little free space. I backed up my photo files, moved other files to an external hard drive to create more space, and crossed my fingers.

David successfully transferred all of my mail files to Mail (which comes on Macs), and everything seemed to be working well.

What neither of us knew was that my mail server had been holding on to ALL of my incoming e-mails since sometime in 2004, so when he got everything set up, all 21,664 e-mails – like an unwelcome blast from the past – downloaded onto my computer. Do you have any idea how long it takes to sort through 21,664 e-mails??? Trust me, you don’t even want to think about it.

Oh, in case you’re wondering about the subject line, my husband was shopping for shingles for the new carport he’s building. No worries: it’s not contagious.

Well, I wish I’d known this was just a sign of a major problem (the computer, not the shingles). My laptop worked great for about a week. Then on the weekend when I was trying to update my iPod (which I love), iTunes quit a couple of times. I still didn’t see the writing on the wall. Yeah, I know, how naive was that?

I won’t traumatize you with the blow by blow, it’s too painful, but will you please join me in a moment of silence for my 12″ silver PowerBook and the unsaved data files?

Thank you. I really appreciate that.

I will say this new MacBook is quite fast. I’m trying to not think about the lost data files.

Can I just tell you what I they told me at the Apple Store? It might save you from a future headache:

All computer hard drives have a 100% chance of failure, it’s just a matter of when.

So I learned the hard way, back up your files, because no matter what computer you’re on, it will fail.

Traveling to Virginia

By Jan | Published: April 19, 2007

Today I headed to Norfolk, Virginia to tape 2 segments for the daytime show, Living the Life. I knew when I accepted the invitation that time timing wasn’t the best, since I just moved into my new studio space, but, hey, sometimes you gotta do what you gotta do. The opportunity was too good to pass up and I’m almost always up for a new experience.

I didn’t have a lot of information beforehand, but I did know that each segment was going to focus on a different one of my books. I had a chance to share my Scrapbook on the Go – Travel Journal process.

I absolutely Love to travel, and have had lots of great opportunities to do that. My Scrapbook on the Go process evolved as I found myself coming home to lots of photographs and grand plans, but didn’t have the time or energy once I got home to actually create the book!

So, my books are all about the process of working quickly, spontaneously, and with whatever supplies are within reach, capturing the energy of the place while you’re still there. It’s a really fun way to work, and I never tire of the fun and variety, and, of course, the finished book to share with anyone who happens to be nearby.

The second segment I taped focused on Altered Board Book Basics and Beyond.

The producer of the show had chosen to focus on a house shaped blank board book, so I worked on some new samples for the show. I made one Coffee House book, to share photos and info on some of my favorite coffee shops that I discover (when traveling), and I also did a series of 3 house shaped books to honor all the work we did (mostly my husband did) during a Major renovation of our kitchen/dining/family room a year or so ago. One of the books was before demolition, one was during, and one was the pretty part of the process as we put everything back together and finished up.

The hotel they put me up in was lovely, everything ran according to schedule, and it was a lot of fun! The next show I know will air on June 26th, check your local listing for Living the Life – probably on the ABC Family Channel.

I liked everything about the experience (except for the heavy make up they put me in for the taping). The hosts were really fun, the production team was friendly and supportive, and it was a good experience all around. My flight was early getting home – do you know how rare that is for me when flying into Charlotte?

Move In Day

By Jan | Published: April 14, 2007

Today’s the day I actually get to move in to my studio at the McColl Center for Visual Arts in downtown Charlotte! I found out in December that I was awarded an 11 month Affiliate Artist position at the non profit center.

I had several emotional ups and downs after I found out about the opportunity. First, as you would expect, I was extremely excited. Then I realized I had 4 months before I actually moved in. Then I took advantage of the time to plan : what would I take with me, what artwork did I want to concentrate on? Then I realized I still had 3 1/2 months before I moved in…….Then I got excited about the experience of having a studio away from my home. Then, I realized I still had 3 1/4 months before I moved in…….it was like I was a kid again, waiting, waiting, waiting.

Surprisingly, just like that, it was April and time to get organized! Yeah right. I never did organize my packing. It just happened as I loaded the truck and my Prius (I love my car) with everything I thought I wanted to have access to right away. As you can see, I didn’t really organize, I just threw stuff in:

Here I am heading in to the McColl for the first time:


I was assigned the amazing Bell Tower studio. Two floors, over 700 square feet total. Yeah, I know, I am incredibly lucky! What a space – here’s the upstairs part of my new studio:


Yes, those amazing windows do have UV shades that can be lowered to block out a lot of the heat from Carolina summer sun.

Here’s a view from the lower floor heading upstairs – you can even see how much natural light is filtering down the stairs:


Well, 3 trips later, everything that I’m moving in (for now) has been dropped off and waiting for me to organize it and start working on new art. I can hardly wait!
