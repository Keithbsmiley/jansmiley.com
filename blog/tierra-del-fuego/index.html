Tierra del Fuego

By Jan | Published: January 31, 2008
One of the major attractions near Ushuaia is Tierra del Fuego National Park
Founded in 1960, this park is located on the Argentina-Chile border, at the southern end of the Andes mountain range. 
I can’t do the park justice, in words or in photographs. It’s a beautiful place, even during the chilly, drizzly time we were there.

We took a hike. It’s a beautiful place. We had been seeing such dry, dusty, flat, and harsh landscapes, that we were both thrilled by all the green around us and the trees. 


After deciding which photos to share today, I couldn’t help but notice something I didn’t realize before. Look at the color in the two photographs above …….. OK? Now look at the color of the yarn I purchased in town later in the day. Yeah, believe it or not, I didn’t notice at the time that this is exactly the color I had been drawn to on the hike. 
I found this wool yarn at a shop in town that carried lots of different hand crafted items. The girl working in the store wasn’t able to answer my detailed questions about the breed of sheep, or what type of dye was used to make this yarn. She did say it came from sheep raised in the region, so I’d like to think that it was dyed with madder root or the South American cochineal bug, since these are the natural sources for red dye. No facts to base that on, but no one to dispute it either! 
Thanks so much for letting me share this amazing trip with you. I’ve had a really good time and appreciate the comments and e-mails you’ve sent me. I love hearing from you guys!
